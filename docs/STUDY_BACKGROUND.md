# Study Background

## Clinical Context

Hospitalized patients outside the intensive care unit represent a broad and clinically heterogeneous population, ranging from low-acuity admissions to patients at substantial risk of clinical deterioration. Even among non-ICU admissions, in-hospital mortality remains a meaningful outcome influenced by age, admission context, comorbidities, and pre-existing medication use.

Renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS) inhibitors, including angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors (ACE inhibitors; ACEi) and angiotensin II receptor blockers (ARBs), are widely prescribed for cardiovascular, renal, and metabolic indications. Beyond blood pressure control, these agents interact with pathways related to vascular tone, inflammatory signaling, endothelial function, and tissue perfusion.

## Rationale

Prior observational studies in hospitalized and population-based settings have reported associations between baseline or early RAAS inhibitor exposure and clinical outcomes in systemic illness, including pneumonia-related outcomes and viral respiratory infections. During the COVID-19 pandemic, large observational studies supported the clinical safety of continued RAAS inhibitor use during acute illness and did not show increased risk of infection or adverse outcomes.

Much of the prior evidence has focused on specific diseases, ICU cohorts, or pandemic-related populations. General adult non-ICU hospital admissions remain an important setting for real-world evidence research because they represent a large, heterogeneous inpatient population in which absolute risk differences may be clinically interpretable even when event rates are low.

## Study Objective

The primary objective is to evaluate whether early RAAS inhibitor exposure is associated with differences in in-hospital mortality among adult, non-ICU hospital admissions in MIMIC-IV.

Secondary objectives include:

- Estimating absolute risk differences using average marginal effects
- Evaluating age-specific risk differences
- Comparing relative and absolute effect measures within a unified modeling framework

## Research Question

Among adult, non-ICU hospital admissions in MIMIC-IV, is early exposure to RAAS inhibitors associated with differences in in-hospital mortality after multivariable adjustment?
